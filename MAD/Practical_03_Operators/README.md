# Practical 3: Arithmetic and Logical Operators

## What are Operators?
Think of operators as the action words or "verbs" in programming. They tell the computer to take two pieces of data and *do something* with them.

### 1. Arithmetic Operators
These are just like the basic math you learned in school!
* **Plus `+`**: Adds two numbers together.
* **Minus `-`**: Subtracts one from the other.
* **Multiply `*`** (we use the star symbol instead of 'x'): Multiplies them.
* **Divide `/`**: Divides one by the other.

### 2. Logical Operators
Sometimes you aren't doing math; you are answering "Yes/No" (True/False) questions and making decisions based on combinations of conditions.
* **AND (`&&`)**: Imagine going out. You will ONLY take an umbrella if "It is raining" AND "You are walking outside". If *both* things are true, the result is true. Otherwise, it is false.
* **OR (`||`)**: Imagine buying snacks. You will buy popcorn if "You have cash" OR "You have a credit card". As long as *at least one* of those is true, you get popcorn!
